D413 – Review Questions – Chap 14 Answers 
 
1. What is the name used for the telephone line running between customer 
premises and the Local Central Office? 
o Local Loop or Last Mile. 
2. Where is the Point of Presence (PoP) located? 
o At the Internet Service Provider's (ISP) facility or a central office/data 
center where an ISP connects to the internet backbone. 
3. What does the acronym ISDN represent? 
o Integrated Services Digital Network. 
4. How many channels are there in a BRI-ISDN cable assembly? 
o Three (Two B channels and one D channel). 
5. What is the maximum bandwidth of a BRI-ISDN service? 
o 128 kbps (2 x 64 kbps B channels). 
6. What is the maximum bandwidth for a telephone modem? 
o 56 kbps (V.90/V.92 standards). 
7. What is the purpose of the D channel in an ISDN cable assembly? 
o Signaling and control information. It carries call setup, teardown, and 
other control messages. 
8. What is the maximum theoretical bandwidth for cable Internet access? 
o The maximum theoretical bandwidth for cable Internet access is defined 
by the DOCSIS (Data Over Cable Service Interface Specification) 
standard. The most current version that is being rolled out is DOCSIS 4.0. 
With DOCSIS 4.0, the theoretical maximum bandwidth is: Download: Up 
to 10 Gigabits per second (Gbps). Upload: Up to 6 Gigabits per 
second (Gbps). It's important to note that these are theoretical maximums 
under ideal conditions. Real-world speeds can be affected by factors such 
as: 
1. Network congestion: Cable networks are shared, so performance 
can vary during peak usage times. 
2. Provider implementation: ISPs may not deploy the full theoretical 
speeds of DOCSIS 4.0 immediately or universally. 
3. Customer equipment: Your modem and home networking 
equipment need to support DOCSIS 4.0 to achieve these speeds. 
4. Asymmetrical speeds: While DOCSIS 4.0 significantly boosts 
upload speeds and allows for more symmetrical services, 
traditionally, cable internet has been asymmetrical, with download 
speeds much higher than upload speeds. 
9. What factors influence the speed of a cable Internet service connection? 
o DOCSIS version, number of bonded channels, network congestion, 
distance from the cable modem termination system (CMTS), quality 
of in-home cabling, and ISP provisioning. 
10. What is the difference between a T1 and a fractional T1 line? 
o A T1 line provides a full 1.544 Mbps of bandwidth. A fractional T1 line 
provides a portion of that bandwidth, typically in increments of 64 kbps 
(e.g., 256 kbps, 512 kbps), by using only some of the available channels. 
11. How many T1 lines make up a T3 line? 
o 28 T1 lines. 
12. Which has the higher bandwidth: a T1 connection or a telephone modem 
connection? 
o A T1 connection (1.544 Mbps vs. 56 kbps). 
13. Which has the higher bandwidth: SONET or ISDN? 
o SONET (Synchronous Optical Network) has significantly higher bandwidth 
as it is a fiber-optic transmission system designed for high-speed, long-
distance communication, whereas ISDN is a relatively low-bandwidth 
digital telephone service. 
14. Which has the higher bandwidth: T1 or X.25? 
o T1 (1.544 Mbps vs. typically much lower speeds for X.25, which was a 
packet-switching protocol often used over lower-speed leased lines). 
15. What high-speed technology is used for long-distance communication, 
especially overseas via underwater? 
o Fiber-optic cables (often utilizing technologies like SONET/SDH or 
DWDM). 
16. Which has a highest bandwidth: ISDN or consumer satellite service? 
o Generally, modern consumer satellite service offers higher bandwidth 
than ISDN, although satellite latency can be an issue. 
17. What does the acronym CSU/DSU represent? 
o Channel Service Unit/Data Service Unit. 
18. What type of media is a CSU/DSU associated with? 
o Digital leased lines (like T1/E1 lines). 
19. Match the following: 
o i. ISDN - C. Two B channels and one D channel 
o ii. DSL - E. Digital telephone line (though more specifically, it uses 
existing telephone lines for high-speed data) 
o iii. SONET - A. Fiber-optic system used for overseas connections 
o iv. T3 - D. Typically 45-Mbps bandwidth 
o v. Telephone modem - B. Typically the slowest connection technology 
20. In what situation is the PPPoE protocol used? 
o When connecting to an ISP via DSL (Digital Subscriber Line), where it 
is used to encapsulate Ethernet frames over PPP, providing authentication 
and session management. 
21. What is the difference between a Virtual Private Network (VPN) and a 
network that uses a T1 line to connect two branch offices? 
o A T1 line provides a dedicated, point-to-point physical connection with a 
guaranteed bandwidth between the two branch offices. It offers inherent 
security because it's a private line. 
o A Virtual Private Network (VPN) creates a secure, encrypted "tunnel" 
over a public network (like the internet). It doesn't provide a dedicated 
physical line, and its bandwidth is dependent on the underlying internet 
connection. VPNs offer flexibility and cost savings by leveraging existing 
internet infrastructure, but rely on encryption for security.
