# Chapter 2 – Review Questions & Answers

## Q1 What are two general classifications of network media?
**A:**
- Guided (or Bounded) Media
- Unguided (or Unbounded) Media

## Q2 Twisted-pair and coaxial cables both use pulsating _____ energy to transmit data.
**A:** Electrical

## Q3 _____ signals have discrete values, while _____ signals have continuous ranges of values.
**A:** Digital signals have discrete values, while analog signals have continuous ranges of values.

## Q4 What is attenuation?
**A:** Attenuation is the loss of signal strength (power) over distance. As a signal travels through a medium, it gets weaker.

## Q5 What is latency?
**A:** Latency is the delay in time from when a signal is transmitted from one point until it is received at another point. It is often measured in milliseconds.

## Q6 Noise and crosstalk are types of _____.
**A:** Interference

## Q7 Differentiate between baseband and broadband transmissions.
**A:**
- **Baseband** transmission uses the entire bandwidth of the cable to transmit a single digital signal at a time. Ethernet is a common example.
- **Broadband** transmission uses analog signaling and divides the bandwidth into multiple channels, allowing multiple signals to be transmitted simultaneously over different frequencies. Cable TV is a common example.

## Q8 List the three modes in which communication can occur.
**A:**
- Simplex
- Half-duplex
- Full-duplex

## Q9 What is the difference between half-duplex and full-duplex communication?
**A:**
- **Half-duplex** allows data to flow in both directions, but only one direction at a time. Devices must take turns transmitting and receiving (e.g., walkie-talkie).
- **Full-duplex** allows data to flow in both directions simultaneously. Devices can transmit and receive at the same time (e.g., telephone conversation).

## Q10 What is the difference between direct current (DC) and alternating current (AC)?
**A:**
- **Direct Current (DC)** flows in only one direction and has a constant voltage polarity (e.g., batteries).
- **Alternating Current (AC)** periodically reverses its direction of flow and changes its voltage polarity (e.g., household electricity).

## Q11 _____ is the opposition to direct current; _____ is the opposition to alternating current.
**A:** Resistance is the opposition to direct current; impedance is the opposition to alternating current.

## Q12 The amount of signal reflected from the end of the cable is called _____.
**A:** Return loss

## Q13 Describe crosstalk.
**A:** Crosstalk is a type of interference that occurs when a signal transmitted on one circuit or wire creates an undesired effect on another adjacent circuit or wire due to electromagnetic induction. It's like "eavesdropping" of signals between wires.

## Q14 List four types of measurement that can be taken on twisted-pair cable to measure the effects of crosstalk.
**A:**
- Near-End Crosstalk (NEXT)
- Far-End Crosstalk (FEXT)
- Power Sum Near-End Crosstalk (PSNEXT)
- Power Sum Far-End Crosstalk (PSFEXT)

## Q15 List the four categories of the IEEE 802.3 classification.
**A:**
- 10BASE-T (Fast Ethernet)
- 100BASE-T (Gigabit Ethernet)
- 1000BASE-T (10 Gigabit Ethernet)
- 10GBASE-T, 40GBASE-T, 100GBASE-T (Higher speed Ethernets)

## Q16 RG-58 is also referred to as _____.
**A:** Thinnet (or 10BASE2)

## Q17 The size of a conductor's diameter is described by what rating?
**A:** American Wire Gauge (AWG) rating.

## Q18 Which conductor has a larger diameter, one that has a 22 AWG rating or one that has a 24 AWG rating?
**A:** A conductor with a 22 AWG rating has a larger diameter. Lower AWG numbers indicate a larger wire diameter.

## Q19 What type of coaxial cable is recommended by the EIA/TIA 570 residential communication standard?
**A:** RG-6 coaxial cable.

## Q20 Which type of coaxial cable is referred to as thicknet?
**A:** RG-8 (or 10BASE5).

## Q21 How many categories of twisted-pair cables exist?
**A:** Seven main categories are widely recognized: Category 3, 5, 5e, 6, 6A, 7, and 8.

## Q22 What is the maximum frequency rating of Category 6 UTP cable?
**A:** 250 MHz.

## Q23 List the two main cable termination standards for twisted-pair cable.
**A:**
- T568A
- T568B

## Q24 What type of connector is used for UTP cable?
**A:** RJ-45 (Registered Jack 45).

## Q25 List and describe five wiring faults that can occur from incorrect wiring.
**A:**
- **Open (or Break):** A complete break in one or more conductors, preventing signal flow.
- **Short:** Two or more conductors are accidentally touching, creating an unintended electrical path.
- **Crossed Pair:** The conductors within a twisted pair are reversed at one end of the cable.
- **Reversed Pair:** The wires in one pair are connected to the pins for another pair, but the order within the pair is maintained.
- **Split Pair:** One wire from each of two different pairs is swapped at both ends, resulting in correct pin-out but improper twisting, which increases crosstalk.
