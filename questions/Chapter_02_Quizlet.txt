D413 – Review Questions – Chap 2 Answers 
 
1. What are two general classifications of network media? 
o Guided (or Bounded) Media 
o Unguided (or Unbounded) Media 
2. Twisted-pair and coaxial cables both use pulsating electrical energy to transmit 
data. 
3. Digital signals have discrete values, while analog signals have continuous 
ranges of values. 
4. What is attenuation? Attenuation is the loss of signal strength (power) over 
distance. As a signal travels through a medium, it gets weaker. 
5. What is latency? Latency is the delay in time from when a signal is transmitted 
from one point until it is received at another point. It is often measured in 
milliseconds. 
6. Noise and crosstalk are types of interference. 
7. Differentiate between baseband and broadband transmissions. 
o Baseband transmission uses the entire bandwidth of the cable to 
transmit a single digital signal. It is a digital signaling technique that uses 
the medium for a single, high-speed signal at a time (though multiple 
signals can be multiplexed using time-division multiplexing). Ethernet is a 
common example. 
o Broadband transmission uses analog signaling and divides the 
bandwidth of the cable into multiple channels, allowing multiple signals or 
data streams to be transmitted simultaneously over different frequencies. 
Cable TV is a common example. 
8. List the three modes in which communication can occur. 
o Simplex 
o Half-duplex 
o Full-duplex 
9. What is the difference between half-duplex and full-duplex communication? 
o Half-duplex communication allows data to flow in both directions, but 
only one direction at a time. Devices must take turns transmitting and 
receiving. (e.g., walkie-talkie). 
o Full-duplex communication allows data to flow in both directions 
simultaneously. Devices can transmit and receive at the same time. (e.g., 
telephone conversation). 
10. What is the difference between direct current (DC) and alternating current 
(AC)? 
o Direct Current (DC) flows in only one direction and has a constant 
voltage polarity. (e.g., batteries). 
o Alternating Current (AC) periodically reverses its direction of flow and 
changes its voltage polarity. (e.g., household electricity). 
11. Resistance is the opposition to direct current; impedance is the opposition to 
alternating current. 
12. The amount of signal reflected from the end of the cable is called return loss. 
13. Describe crosstalk. Crosstalk is a type of interference that occurs when a signal 
transmitted on one circuit or wire creates an undesired effect on another adjacent 
circuit or wire due to electromagnetic induction. It's like "eavesdropping" of 
signals between wires. 
14. List four types of measurement that can be taken on twisted-pair cable to 
measure the effects of crosstalk. 
o Near-End Crosstalk (NEXT) 
o Far-End Crosstalk (FEXT) 
o Power Sum Near-End Crosstalk (PSNEXT) 
o Power Sum Far-End Crosstalk (PSFEXT) 
15. List the four categories of the IEEE 802.3 classification. Categories refers to 
the different standards that specify media or speeds. The most common 
classifications within 802.3 are: 
o 10BASE-T (Fast Ethernet) 
o 100BASE-T (Gigabit Ethernet) 
o 1000BASE-T (10 Gigabit Ethernet) 
o 10GBASE-T, 40GBASE-T, 100GBASE-T, etc. (Higher speed Ethernets) 
16. RG-58 is also referred to as Thinnet (or 10BASE2). 
17. The size of a conductor’s diameter is described by what rating? American Wire 
Gauge (AWG) rating. 
18. Which conductor has a larger diameter, one that has a 22 AWG rating or one that 
has a 24 AWG rating? A conductor with a 22 AWG rating has a larger diameter. 
(Lower AWG numbers indicate a larger wire diameter). 
19. What type of coaxial cable is recommended by the EIA/TIA 570 residential 
communication standard? RG-6 coaxial cable. 
20. Which type of coaxial cable is referred to as thicknet? RG-8 (or 10BASE5). 
21. How many categories of twisted-pair cables exist? While new standards and 
drafts emerge, generally seven main categories are widely recognized and used 
for data networking: Category 3, 5, 5e, 6, 6A, 7, and 8.  
22. What is the maximum frequency rating of Category 6 UTP cable? 250 MHz. 
23. List the two main cable termination standards for twisted-pair cable. 
o T568A 
o T568B 
24. What type of connector is used for UTP cable? RJ-45 (Registered Jack 45). 
25. List and describe five wiring faults that can occur from incorrect wiring. 
o Open (or Break): A complete break in one or more of the cable's 
conductors, preventing signal flow. 
o Short: Two or more conductors (or a conductor and shield) are 
accidentally touching, creating an unintended electrical path. 
o Crossed Pair: The conductors within a twisted pair are reversed at one 
end of the cable (e.g., blue/white blue is connected to white blue/blue). 
o Reversed Pair: The wires in one pair are connected to the pins for 
another pair, but the order within the pair is maintained (e.g., pin 1 and 2 
of pair 1 are connected to pin 3 and 6 which should be for pair 2). 
o Split Pair: Two pairs are incorrectly wired, where one wire from each of 
two different pairs is swapped at both ends, resulting in the correct pin-out 
but improper twisting, which increases crosstalk.
