# Chapter 4 – Review Questions & Answers

## Q1 A(n) _____ is an electromagnetic wave of a set frequency that is used to carry data in radio wave- and microwave-based networks.
**A:** Carrier wave.

## Q2 What is an SSID?
**A:** An SSID (Service Set Identifier) is a unique identifier assigned to a WLAN (Wireless Local Area Network). It acts as the name of the wireless network, allowing devices to identify and connect to it.

## Q3 What is modulation?
**A:** Modulation is the process of superimposing information (data) onto a carrier wave by varying one or more of its properties, such as its amplitude, frequency, or phase.

## Q4 A simple radio broadcast consists of a(n) _____, which generates a carrier wave, and a(n) _____, which receives a carrier wave.
**A:** A transmitter generates a carrier wave; a receiver receives a carrier wave.

## Q5 What is a channel?
**A:** A channel is a specific frequency or frequency range within a larger band used for communication. In wireless networking, different channels are used to avoid interference between devices or networks.

## Q6 What is the main disadvantage to infrared?
**A:** Infrared requires line of sight between the transmitter and receiver, has limited range, and is susceptible to interference from bright light sources.

## Q7 What are the two major classifications of antennae?
**A:** Omnidirectional and unidirectional (or directional).

## Q8 Which of the following antenna types is not used in point-to-point links? A. Flat panel B. Omni C. Parabolic D. Yagi
**A:** B. Omni. Omnidirectional antennas broadcast in all directions, whereas point-to-point links require focused, directional beams.

## Q9 Which antenna style was developed with aesthetics in mind?
**A:** The patch antenna (or flat panel) style was developed with aesthetics in mind.

## Q10 What is spread spectrum?
**A:** Spread spectrum is a technique used in wireless communication to transmit data over a wide range of frequencies rather than a single frequency. This makes the signal more resistant to interference, jamming, and eavesdropping.

## Q11 What is the maximum range of Bluetooth 5.0?
**A:** Approximately 240 meters (800 feet) in an open-air environment, though this can vary based on obstacles and power output.

## Q12 Why is frequency hopping used?
**A:** Frequency hopping is used to avoid interference, increase security, and improve resistance to jamming. By rapidly switching frequencies, it makes it difficult for unauthorized parties to intercept or disrupt communication.

## Q13 The spread-spectrum technique that transmits data on multiple channels sequentially is called _____.
**A:** Frequency Hopping Spread Spectrum (FHSS).

## Q14 The _____ transmission technique uses the 5-GHz frequency and can achieve data rates as high as 54 Mbps.
**A:** OFDM (Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiplexing).

## Q15 List the frequency range for each of the three U-NII classifications.
**A:**
- **U-NII-1 (Lower Band):** 5.150–5.250 GHz
- **U-NII-2 (Middle Band):** 5.250–5.350 GHz
- **U-NII-3 (Upper Band):** 5.725–5.825 GHz

## Q16 What frequency does IEEE 802.11g specify?
**A:** 2.4 GHz.

## Q17 What frequency does IEEE 802.11n specify?
**A:** Both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz.

## Q18 What does the acronym MIMO represent?
**A:** Multiple-Input Multiple-Output.

## Q19 What is MIMO wireless technology?
**A:** MIMO uses multiple antennas at both the transmitter and receiver to improve communication performance. By sending and receiving multiple data streams simultaneously over the same radio channel, it increases data throughput and signal reliability.

## Q20 Describe the IEEE 802.11ac standard.
**A:** IEEE 802.11ac operates exclusively in the 5 GHz band. It introduced wider channels (up to 160 MHz), more spatial streams (up to 8), and MU-MIMO capabilities, allowing for much higher theoretical data rates (up to several Gbps) and better performance in dense environments. It is often called "Gigabit Wi-Fi."

## Q21 Which media access method does the 802.11 wireless standard specify?
**A:** CSMA/CA (Carrier Sense Multiple Access with Collision Avoidance).

## Q22 What is the difference between CSMA/CA and CSMA/CD?
**A:**
- **CSMA/CD** (used in wired Ethernet): Devices listen to the network and if they detect a collision after transmitting, they stop, wait a random amount of time, and try again.
- **CSMA/CA** (used in wireless 802.11): Devices try to avoid collisions before they happen by listening to the channel and sending a small RTS (Request to Send) signal, then waiting for a CTS (Clear to Send) from the receiver before transmitting data.

## Q23 What is the maximum achievable distance of a Bluetooth device?
**A:** Around 10 meters (33 feet) for Class 2 devices, and up to 100 meters (330 feet) for Class 1 devices.

## Q24 What is the data rate of the Bluetooth 3.0 standard?
**A:** Up to 24 Mbps, achieved by leveraging Wi-Fi (802.11) radio for high-speed data transfer after an initial Bluetooth connection was established.

## Q25 _____ technology is based on radio waves connecting to designated areas referred to as cells.
**A:** Cellular technology.

## Q26 Long Term Evolution (LTE) is also known as _____.
**A:** 4G LTE.

## Q27 What is a geosynchronous orbit?
**A:** A geosynchronous orbit is an orbit around the Earth where a satellite's orbital period matches Earth's rotation period. A geostationary orbit is a specific type that is circular and directly above the equator, making the satellite appear stationary from the ground.

## Q28 What is a disadvantage of satellite communication?
**A:** High latency due to the long distance signals must travel to and from the satellite. Other disadvantages include high cost, susceptibility to weather interference, and security challenges.

## Q29 What is an advantage of wireless networking?
**A:** Mobility — users can access network resources from anywhere within the coverage area without physical cables. Other advantages include ease of installation, flexibility, and support for mobile devices.

## Q30 What is the one major disadvantage of wireless technology?
**A:** Security vulnerability. Wireless signals are broadcast over the air, making them more susceptible to interception, eavesdropping, and unauthorized access compared to wired connections.

## Q31 What does the acronym WEP represent?
**A:** Wired Equivalent Privacy.

## Q32 What does the acronym TKIP stand for?
**A:** Temporal Key Integrity Protocol.
