D413 – Review Questions – Chap 12 Answers 
 
1. Converting digital pulses to an analog waveform is called Digital-to-Analog 
(D/A) conversion. 
2. Converting an analog waveform to digital pulses is called Analog-to-Digital 
(A/D) conversion. 
3. Jitter is the variation in the delay of received packets. It can cause distortions 
and drops in real-time communications like voice and video. 
4. The delay of data from its origin to the final destination is called latency. 
5. Factors that contribute to echo include:  
o Impedance mismatches in the network (e.g., at the 2-wire to 4-wire 
conversion point in a hybrid). 
o Acoustic coupling between a speaker and microphone (acoustic echo). 
o Delay in the signal path. 
6. The principal use for MPEG compression is video and audio compression for 
digital multimedia (e.g., streaming, digital TV, DVDs). 
7. The X.25 protocol uses analog signals to transmit data across long distances. It 
was developed in the early years of the telecommunications industry, prior to the 
widespread use of digital signals. An analog transport design for data was 
natural. What makes X.25 unusual when compared with other analog 
technologies is that it uses packet switching rather than permanent, electrical 
connections. Analog signals are routed around the world in packets that often 
follow different routes to reach their destination. X.25 laid the foundation for 
packet-switching technology. X.25 is not commonly used in the United States, but 
many locations overseas are still committed to X.25. The X.25 protocol is limited 
to a maximum data rate of 56 kbps. X.25 is one of the oldest packet switching 
protocols.  
8. A Switched Virtual Circuit (SVC) is a temporary connection between two devices 
on a network. 
9. Based on what two factors will an ATM switch drop a cell?  
o Congestion (the switch's buffers are full). 
o Errors (the cell header checksum indicates an error, or the cell is 
malformed). 
10. Which VoIP protocol is used to establish and terminate a VoIP session? SIP 
(Session Initiation Protocol). 
11. Which VoIP protocol is used to stream multimedia in real time between two 
network nodes? RTP (Real-time Transport Protocol). 
12. What is another name for bandwidth shaper? Traffic shaper or rate limiter. 
13. What is the purpose of a bandwidth shaper? The purpose of a bandwidth shaper 
is to control the rate at which data is sent over a network link, ensuring that 
data does not exceed a certain bandwidth limit and to smooth out bursts of traffic. 
This helps to prevent network congestion and improve the quality of service for 
certain applications. 
14. What is the purpose of the QoS protocol? The purpose of QoS (Quality of 
Service) protocols is to manage network resources to provide preferential 
treatment to certain types of network traffic (e.g., voice and video) over 
others, ensuring a predictable and satisfactory level of performance for critical 
applications. 
15. What is the most common source of VoIP echo? The most common source of 
VoIP echo is acoustic echo, which occurs when the sound from the speaker's 
earpiece is picked up by their own microphone. Another common source is 
hybrid echo due to impedance mismatches at the conversion between 2-wire 
and 4-wire circuits.
